About Avinash Jha

Avinash Ranjan Jha is a dynamic and educated young politician, thinker, and social activist deeply committed to the regeneration of his home state, Bihar, and the broader Ganga-Yamuna region.

Background and Education:

Born in Prayagraj (Allahabad), Uttar Pradesh, and brought up primarily in Bihar, Avinash holds a Post Graduate Diploma in Management (PGDM/MBA) with specializations in HR and Marketing (2001), along with technical certifications like MCSE and CCNA.

Journey into Public Life:

After a successful corporate career (2001-2008), the devastating Kosi flood tragedy in 2008 spurred him to dedicate his life to public service. He founded the **"Bihar Revival Forum"** ([revive-bihar.blogspot.com](https://revive-bihar.blogspot.com/)) to advocate for solutions, notably the "Adbhut Samriddhi Canal" project.

Political Engagements:

With nearly three decades of political involvement (since 1995), Avinash has worked with the Indian National Congress (INC) and the Rashtriya Janata Dal (RJD), holding key state-level positions like State Secretary and General Secretary in the latter. He has extensive grassroots experience, has conducted in-depth socio-political studies, and contributed to national campaigns like the Bharat Jodo Yatra and as an AICC Observer.

Core Philosophy:

His work is driven by the philosophy of **"Development through data and technology-based politics."** He advocates for leveraging modern tools to address Bihar's chronic issues and unlock its potential.

Vision for Bihar:

Avinash envisions a **holistic regeneration** of Bihar – making it self-reliant, progressive, equitable, prosperous, culturally vibrant, and sustainable, focusing on Education, Employment, Infrastructure, Good Governance, and Social Justice.

Beyond politics, he holds an interest in Astrology and Numerology (@astroavinash), adding a philosophical dimension to his pragmatic approach.

The Book - Bihar Rebirth: A Roadmap

"Bihar Rebirth: A Roadmap" by Avinash Ranjan Jha is more than an analysis; it's a comprehensive **blueprint for action** for the state's holistic regeneration. It confronts Bihar's harsh realities – floods, migration, unemployment, ailing systems, corruption – and offers bold, integrated, and actionable solutions.

  • **Implementation & Future Vision (Part 6 & Supplement):** Focuses on enablers like sustainable energy (Solar Mission), good governance (anti-corruption), digital leapfrogging (digital education, telemedicine), economic diversification (Farm-to-Kitchen, E-commerce, Agri-Exports, Twin Cities), strategic investor outreach, and addressing systemic issues.

Vision & Roadmap for Bihar's Rebirth

The vision is for a **holistically regenerated Bihar** – prosperous, equitable, educated, healthy, sustainable, culturally vibrant, well-governed, and globally connected. The roadmap is an integrated strategy combining large-scale projects with systemic reforms and innovative ideas.

I. Mithila Samriddhi Panchasutri Pariyojana (5-Point Plan):

  1. **Adbhut Samriddhi Canal:** 500 km canal for permanent flood control, irrigation (2-2.5M Ha), 500+ MW hydro, inland navigation (connecting to NW1), groundwater recharge. *Integrates existing infrastructure and uses innovative silt management.*
  2. **Special Economic Zones (SEZs):** In all 9 divisions, focused on regional strengths (agro-processing, textiles, IT), creating millions of local jobs. *PPP model preferred.*
  3. **Pag-Pag Pokhar Machh Makhan:** Reviving/creating thousands of ponds for fisheries (Target: 1.5M+ tonnes), Makhana industry (Target: ₹5000 Cr), water conservation, rural livelihood.
  4. **IT City (Hub & Spoke):** World-class green IT hub (near Patna) + **Satellite IT Parks in every constituency**. Target global IT/IoT market (125M jobs by 2030), position Bihar as 'low-cost' hub (Target: 1-2M professionals), attract NRB talent.
  5. **Janaki Spiritual Circuit:** Developing 11+ sites in Mithila, grand Sita statue, linking with Ramayan circuit, boosting tourism and local arts.

II. Foundational Enablers & Systemic Reforms:

  • **Sustainable Energy & Environment:** Aggressive Solar Mission (10,000 MW+ target - utility, rooftop, canal-top, pumps), renewable diversification (wind, biomass, small hydro), massive Rainwater Harvesting, Afforestation (25%+ target), Pollution Control, Waste Management.
  • **Good Governance & Anti-Corruption:** E-governance, mandatory digital payments, transparency (dashboards, RTI), strict penalties (special courts, asset forfeiture), protection for honest officials/whistleblowers.
  • **Digital Leapfrogging (Education & Health):** Modernized universities (IT, GenAI, Open Source, Foreign Languages), state-wide Digital Learning Platform ('Bihar Vidya Vahini') with quality content (Nursery-University) accessible via Smart Classrooms/TVs in all schools, state-wide Telemedicine Network ('Arogya Kendra') with trained staff & remote doctors (incl. NRBs, AI support).
  • **Economic Diversification & Global Linkages:** 'Farm-to-Kitchen' scheme (logistics, app), E-commerce Empowerment for rural artisans (training, CFCs, global platforms), 'Twin Cities' program with Europe (cultural/experiential tourism), Direct Agri-Exports (fruits/veg to Europe via air cargo), Strategic Investor Outreach (targeted campaigns, personal invites, 24x7 helpline, 'Invest Bihar' agency).
  • **Addressing Sensitive Issues:** Ensuring Electoral Roll Integrity (data analysis, verification), open dialogue on Regional Aspirations (e.g., Mithila state demand) focusing on balanced development.

III. Implementation Strategy:

  • **Bihar Revival Forum/Authority:** An empowered, multi-stakeholder body for coordination and monitoring.
  • **Political Will & Consensus:** Sustained commitment beyond party lines.
  • **Efficient Bureaucracy:** Accountable and capable administration.
  • **Funding:** Mobilizing resources (Central grants/Special Status, State budget, Intl. loans, PPP, Jharkhand dues).
  • **Jan Bhagidari:** Making regeneration a people's movement.

This integrated approach aims to transform Bihar by 2035-2045, leveraging its strengths and tackling its weaknesses head-on.
