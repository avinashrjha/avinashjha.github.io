About Avinash Jha

Avinash Ranjan Jha is a dynamic and educated young politician, thinker, and social activist deeply committed to the regeneration of his home state, Bihar, and the broader Ganga-Yamuna region.

Background and Education:

Born in Prayagraj (Allahabad), Uttar Pradesh, and brought up primarily in Bihar, Avinash holds a Post Graduate Diploma in Management (PGDM/MBA) with specializations in HR and Marketing (2001), along with technical certifications like MCSE and CCNA.

Journey into Public Life:

After a successful corporate career (2001-2008), the devastating Kosi flood tragedy in 2008 spurred him to dedicate his life to public service. He founded the "Bihar Revival Forum" ([revive-bihar.blogspot.com](https://revive-bihar.blogspot.com/)) to advocate for solutions, notably the "Adbhut Samriddhi Canal" project detailed in his book.

Political Engagements:

With nearly three decades of political involvement (since 1995), Avinash has worked with the Indian National Congress (INC) and the Rashtriya Janata Dal (RJD), holding key state-level positions like State Secretary and General Secretary in the latter. He has extensive grassroots experience, has conducted in-depth socio-political studies, and contributed to national campaigns like the Bharat Jodo Yatra and as an AICC Observer.

Core Philosophy:

His work is driven by the philosophy of "Development through data and technology-based politics." He advocates for leveraging modern tools to address Bihar's chronic issues and unlock its potential.

Vision for Bihar:

Avinash envisions a holistic regeneration of Bihar – making it self-reliant, progressive, equitable, prosperous, culturally vibrant, and sustainable, focusing on Education, Employment, Infrastructure, Good Governance, and Social Justice.

Beyond politics, he holds an interest in Astrology and Numerology (@astroavinash), adding a philosophical dimension to his pragmatic approach.

Vision & Roadmap for Bihar's Revival

The vision is for a holistically regenerated Bihar – prosperous, equitable, educated, healthy, sustainable, culturally vibrant, well-governed, and globally connected. The roadmap is an integrated strategy combining large-scale projects with systemic reforms and innovative ideas.

I. Mithila Samriddhi Panchasutri Pariyojana (5-Point Plan):

  1. Adbhut Samriddhi Canal: A revolutionary 500 km canal for permanent flood control, massive irrigation, hydropower, inland navigation, and groundwater recharge.
  2. Special Economic Zones (SEZs): Modern industrial zones in every division for balanced growth and millions of jobs.
  3. Pag-Pag Pokhar Machh Makhan: Reviving traditional water bodies for rural prosperity (fisheries, Makhana).
  4. IT City (Hub & Spoke): World-class green IT hub + Satellite IT Parks across Bihar to capture global opportunities and reverse brain drain.
  5. Janaki Spiritual Circuit: Developing Mithila's cultural/religious heritage for tourism and economic growth.

II. Foundational Enablers & Systemic Reforms:

  • Sustainable Energy & Environment: Massive solar mission (10,000 MW+), renewable diversification, rainwater harvesting, afforestation, pollution control.
  • Good Governance & Anti-Corruption: Transparency, E-governance, strict penalties, protection for honest officials.
  • Digital Leapfrogging (Education & Health): Modernized universities (IT, GenAI, Open Source, Foreign Languages), Digital Learning Platform ('Bihar Vidya Vahini') for all schools, Statewide Telemedicine Network ('Arogya Kendra').
  • Economic Diversification & Global Linkages: 'Farm-to-Kitchen' scheme, E-commerce Empowerment for rural artisans, 'Twin Cities' program, Direct Agri-Exports, Strategic Investor Outreach.
  • Addressing Sensitive Issues: Ensuring Electoral Roll Integrity, open dialogue on Regional Aspirations.

III. Implementation Strategy:

  • Bihar Revival Forum/Authority: An empowered, multi-stakeholder body for coordination.
  • Political Will & Consensus: Sustained commitment beyond party lines.
  • Efficient Bureaucracy: Accountable administration.
  • Funding: Mobilizing resources (Central grants/Special Status, State, Intl. loans, PPP).
  • Mass Awareness & Jan Bhagidari: Making regeneration a people's movement.

This integrated approach aims to transform Bihar by 2035-2045, leveraging its strengths and tackling its weaknesses head-on.
